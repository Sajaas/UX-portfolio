Case Study

Construct & Combat

During three intense weeks we designed a card game which can be played online through Tabletop Simulator.

Download Print & Play Tabletop Simulator
The problem
  • Challenge: create a tabletop game which reflects the theme and features several game modes
  • Client: World Beyond War
  • School: Changemaker Educations
  • Duration: 3 weeks
My roles
  • Game knowledge
  • Design
  • Implementer
  • Facilitator
  • TechWiz
Tools
  • Miro
  • Playingcards.io
  • Tabletop Simulator
  • Photoshop
  • Illustrator
The brief
  • 1-4 players
  • Feature PvP, PvE, solo and Co-op modes
  • 25-30 mins per game
  • Easy rules & setup, 10-15 min onboarding
  • Engaging and replayable
  • Carry learning points
  • Be playable online
  • Print & Play files
  • Sell sheet
  • Reflect the theme assigned clearly

    • Theme: The effects to the people when governments invest in warfare instead of infrastructure, and vice versa.
Teamwork & the process

The team consisted of members with very different levels of game experience. We worked remotely during this project, and it taught us a lot about collaborating online.

We used Miro as our virtual whiteboard and added all the information and ideas relevant to the brief. We had check-ins and check-outs to make sure everyone was on the same page, used Kanban Boards and Dot Voting.

We decided on Design Thinking as the main process for this challenge.

Empathize & Define

We researched both the client and the cost of war. Based on the theme and needs of the client, we defined the goal of the game, target audience and motivations for the player.

We specified game theme ideas and dot voted to pic our top 2 contestants and pitched the idea to a group of classmates and our teacher to validate our assumptions.

Ideating & prototyping

I suggested to aim for a card game, so it will be less overwhelming to new players. After the team agreed, we ideated about the win condition and gameplay.

Our first prototype was a game room on Playingcards.io. I created an Excel sheet with suggested card abilities and exported CSV files to Playingcards.io so we easily could try out concepts with minimal design. This made it easy to update the cards as we iterated.

Playtesting & Implementation


The gameplay was relatively smooth, and we got into it pretty quickly

The simple structure of the game, I think, was quite inviting

I thought it was pretty clever, keeping it simple but still an effective set of things happening during the game

We had the opportunity to ask the clients about their expectations and managed to get a clearer picture of their vision and validate our project. Throughout the whole project we have had continuous testing to isolate any problems and address them.

When we had a nearly finished product, I designed the card face templates in Photoshop, defined the variables, and imported data sets through CSV files. I created a play area in Tabletop Simulator and imported all of our assets.

I designed the rulebook, cheat cards, and Print and Play documents. The rest of the team created the sell sheet and the presentation. We then updated all the files, so they contained the latest updated information.

Presentation & Feedback

We pitched our game idea to the representatives of World Beyond War, and they were impressed with our product presentation and that we fulfilled all the demands.

Key takeaways

There was pros and cons work in a team with such divided game knowledge. What some of us felt was very natural demanded thorough explanation for others, which forced us focus on creating a basic game with easy rules, inviting structures, and low entry barrier.

Tweaking gameplay
If we had more time, I would incorporate more advanced mechanics and lower the randomness. I would add a detailed narrative, and include several great ideas from the presentation and playtesting sessions.

Agile methodology
During the project, we would have benefitted from assigning a Facilitator or Team Leader to curate the discussions and speaking time. Using Through Lightning Decision Jams and Dot Voting we would have worked faster more focused on our brief.

Team dynamics
Working remotely does not allow you to read the subtle social cues, which makes it harder to understand when someone wants to talk. Assigning speaking time for all the team members would allow us to explore more ideas, get valuable feedback, and would probably help us make faster decisions.
