Case Study

Everslaught

As part of the publishing team at Fast Travel Games, I conducted multiple UX tests for our publishing title Everslaught Invasion, a co-op action game.

TUTORIAL & ONBOARDING

I tested the tutorial and onboarding, in solo mode. The goal was to identify any usability issues, as well as fun stoppers, and give suggestions on how to improve it.

Top insights and suggestions from the first round of testing include:

  • While the players enjoyed the action & movement, they struggled with understanding the tutorial at times
  • Teaching players to use the hook to go high up was insufficient
  • Players could walk past important UI screens without seeing them
  • Players felt the enemies had an unfair & unexpected range
  • Hitting enemies felt off
  • Not knowing the goal (level & game) makes the game less enjoyable
  • In the ship, the selections are too spread out and it's annoying to move between them
  • Players thought the game felt repetitive and that the level dragged on
  • Players should be able to pause if they play solo
  • Unclear feedback or goals made players frustrated and confused
  • Players need more audio feedback, such as enemy footsteps and start of waves
  • Texts needed to be revisited, to be shorter and clearer, and changed from justified to left aligned
