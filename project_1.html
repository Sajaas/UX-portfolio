CheckMate

We had 3 school days to create an educational app that will improve learning and add value for students between 9-25.

The problem

A web platform or Mobile app that will improve learning and add value for students between 9-25, and can compete with existing apps.

Research & empathy

At first we created assumptions through GAINS and PAINS, where we dot-voted on what we thought was the most logical problems.

We split into two groups, Research (who looked into current solutions and competitors) and Empathy. I was part of the Empathy team who created assumptions, an empathy map, made an interview script from the assumptions, and interviewed the target audience, where one person interviewed, another transcribed and the last jotted down questions and insights.

Narrowing the scope

After the interviews we sorted the insights into themes, and defined the problem statements. We decided that we could focus on three main features, and dot voted to decide the most important problems.

We wrote the problem statements as user stories and split into three smaller teams to rapidly ideate.

"As a student, I want to be able to follow my progress in order to feel motivated"

"As a student, I want to have a clear structure, so that I know what's expected of me"

"As a student, I want the possibility to choose how to learn, so that I can rationalise my studies"

Ideating & prototyping

Every group ideated, explained the ideas and sorted them into themes. From these themes we could identify features that could solve our main problems, with gamification as a main focus.

When we defined what features each user story would need to be fulfilled, we rapidly created simple wireframes and mapped the user journey. Using Marvel Pop, we quickly created a low-fi clickable prototype.

Testing & feedback

We showed our low-fi prototype, and were surprised how easy people could navigate through our somewhat messy wireframes.

We asked the user to accomplish certain goals, like "navigate to the current subject" or "what's the status of your math progress?". We got a bunch of insights from the testing and corrected the usability accordingly for the hi-fi prototype.

Hi-fi prototype & iteration

We split into two groups, the Figma team, and the Research2.0 team. While the Figma team created the hi-fi prototype based on the low-fi wireframes and user feedback, the research group looked in to the features one last time to make sure that we really focused on the right things.

I was on the Figma team, and we managed to conduct another testing round, update the prototype according to the feedback, and start on our presentation.

Presentation & reflection

I pre-recorded a walkthrough of our app, and it was integrated to our presentation. We focused on the brief, our problem statements, the process and challenges. It was a concise presentation of our projects key points, and it was positively met by our teacher and mentor.

So what could we do better? Well, a whole lot. From the start, we should've decided on team roles, what processes and tools to use, set time references, created less messy wireframes, not lose sight of the MVP... But we learned much during this unorganized assignment, and let go of the unhelpful practices that we experienced during our first project.
